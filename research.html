Payroll Tax Incidence at the Individual and Firm Level: Evidence from Spain
[Draft available soon]

This paper examines the incidence of employer-side payroll taxes through a reform in Spain that introduced, and later repealed, a tax reduction for older workers on open-ended contracts and with certain seniority at the firm. Using administrative Social Security data and matched employer-employee records, I apply dynamic difference-in-differences models at both the individual and firm levels.
